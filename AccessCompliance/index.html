INTRODUCTION

Introduction to "Visage of Time" and Artist statement

Audio - Introduction and Artist statement

“Visage of Time” is an earnest attempt at grasping the concept of time, through the vision and hands of an illustrator, invited to be examined by a multitude of senses.

“Time is a measurement of motion. Anything that measures time is a clock.”

At the center of the exhibition is a pair of videos with set duration. A timelapse, a series of snapshots taken at an exact interval of 1 second after every brushstroke. Still images that the mind filled in the blanks to form an illusion of motion. Now playing back at the speed of 10 snapshots per second.

The top video measured the streamlined path of time. Each frame progressed a set interval from the last, obvious and true in the way you can observe it grow and be refined. From a sketch, to colors, to a rough illustration.

The bottom video, however, is the difference that lay between those 2 frames. It showed the path of each new brushstroke, and of only that brushstroke. A snapshot of a movement, isolated from its surrounding context. A clock that runs without its hands, yet you can hear its ticks.

Without the context of the first video, the second transforms into disconnected movements, regular in interval. It is a measurement of motion.

A time, and a clock to measure it.

The video is aided with use of audio descriptions and closed captions as part of the artistic practice.

On the side, accompanying the piece is a tactile print of the final illustration. With attention placed in texture to bring out areas of interest, and braille typeface where text used to be. It serves as another method to follow along the timed piece, by tracing your hand in its grooves and see, without eyes, how the movement of time could have felt like.

Captured in Still (Tactile print), 2025

Captured in Still - Tactile print model

3D render of the model, with the light source moves in a circular pattern, creating sundial movement around the raised braille

Audio - Captured in Still model detailed description

The tactile print is created using a grayscale version of the altered final illustration, removing some details to be easier to read, as well as replacing the original text with braille. Then translated into a height map with its darker areas becoming higher, and lighter lower.

Areas of interest are painted using a heavily textured brush, leaving small grainy patches akin to dried streaky uneven thick paint in acrylic painting. This resulted in noticeable textures that can be felt for specific things, which include: People and animals, and the items sundials, the tent, and a clock gear. The outline of objects are cleanly separated by height.

The print depicts a lively marketplace from a diagonal top-down view. In the bottom left corner, a wooden crate next to 5 people in long robes, standing roughly in a circle, with the farthest right holding a long thin clock arm.

Directly up from them are 4 people. The 2 in the middle are sitting down, with large sundials in an elliptical with a triangle shape in front. Behind them is a large ox-like cattle, boxy frame and small head, being led by a person.

The large tent is to the right of them. 2 diagonal wings leading inwards to a point high out of frame. 3 large staff holding the tent up. The merchant stands to the left of the middle staff, which is decorated by a sphere and hanging wares. Below the merchant are a pair of parent and child, the child holding a large bundle.

To the right of the pair is another ox-like creature with horns, carrying fabric and wares on its back. A person behind and infront of it. The one in front carrying a large beam and being led by a small child, pointing to the right.

Behind the ox and right of the tent is a large lady and a child hiding behind her, shadowed by the fabric banner.

The bottom right half of the print is a very large pocket watch. The ellipse with small elliptical details in the bottom is the clock body exposed mechanism. 3 people are hauling a large gear into it.

The upright ellipse is the lid of the pocket watch, with ladders set behind it and a person sitting on top. Silk banners, in long thin streaky texture leading outwards from the top of the lid, also casting shadows down below.

Multiple people stand below the silk banner and half hidden by them. Including a pair kneeling on the ground in the middle, and another pair in the far right edge running towards a person (who is part of the 3 hauling the gear) waving at them.

2 sets of braille.
The first set of braille sits between the lady with the large clock arm and the tent, which reads:
“We dance upon the ticking of clock hands as sun rises and moon falls.”

The second set of braille sits between the pointing child and the clock:
“Ephemeral and fleeting, the march of creation measured by man, in sounds and visuals. Does time march in static. In a medium without movement. In a medium without sounds.”
